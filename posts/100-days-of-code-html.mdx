When you're just getting started with web development, the first thing you'll need to learn is <acronym title="Hyper Text Markup Language">HTML</acronym>. HTML will allow you to structure content on a web page. It's relatively easy to learn the basics, but eventually you may want to learn the nuance involved in deciding which elements to use. While many elements will look and behave the same, many have different semantic meaning. It's important to choose the correct elements so your site is easier for crawlers/indexers like Google, to understand what the important information is. It's also very important for the accessibility of users with additional needs, and may be using a screen reader.

Below is an outline for your first 8 days. Feel free to dig deeper each day by doing the bonus content, and practicing on your own. It's up to you to decide how much you want the skills of a web developer and no magical roadmap can bestow that gift upon you. Do the work, get the reward... now get started!
